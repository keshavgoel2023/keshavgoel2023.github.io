Scholar — Mentorship Platform & GNN based Matching Research

Scholar is a mentorship platform connecting aspiring learners and mentors through structured profiles, sessions, and communication. During its design, a limitation in traditional matching was identified, leading to research on a Graph Neural Network based matching algorithm.

Problem Statement

Modern learners often struggle to find the right mentors due to fragmented platforms, unstructured discovery processes, and poor compatibility matching. Most existing mentorship and talent platforms rely on simple keyword search, profile overlap, or manual filtering, which fail to capture deeper relationships between skills, experience levels, learning goals, and domain hierarchies. As a result, mentor–mentee pairing is often inaccurate, non-personalized, and ineffective — especially for new users with limited profile data (cold-start scenarios).

Literature & Existing Systems

  • Rule-based and keyword-overlap matching widely used
  • Flat embedding similarity between user profiles
  • Skills treated as independent — no hierarchy modeling
  • Limited use of relational or graph structure
  • Weak performance in cold-start scenarios

Research Gap

  • No hierarchy-aware mentor matching frameworks
  • Lack of inductive GNN-based compatibility models
  • Poor generalization to unseen users & new skills
  • No strong cold-start matching solutions

System Methodology

Dataset / Input

Synthetic skill dataset generated using an LLM council approach. Used due to privacy concerns and lack of public mentor-skill graphs.

Matching Model

  • Graph Construction: User–skill–category heterogeneous graph built
  • Embedding Learning: Inductive GraphSAGE multi-hop aggregation
  • Compatibility Prediction: Link prediction via embedding similarity

Results & Analysis

Matching Accuracy ~88%

  • GNN-based hierarchical matching outperformed rule-based baselines
  • Achieved ~88% compatibility prediction accuracy
  • Captured indirect and hierarchical skill relationships effectively
  • Maintained strong performance for cold-start users
